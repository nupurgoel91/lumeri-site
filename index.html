Krishnamurti · Philosopher · The Conditioning Cycle

Described the complete cycle of K→E→M→T→A in which human thought, filtered through memory and conditioning, perpetually recreates the same patterns. Identified the gap between Thought and Action as the only moment where genuine freedom is possible — not through method, but through direct seeing of the pattern as it arises.

Linda Graham · Neuroscientist · The Prefrontal Pause

From neuroscience: the prefrontal cortex — our seat of reflective capacity — has approximately 250 milliseconds to intercept an automatic response arising from the amygdala. That microsecond is physiologically real. It is not metaphor. It is the neural correlate of exactly what Krishnamurti pointed to from outside science.

Federico Faggin · Physicist · The Self-Knowing Act

The physicist behind the microprocessor described consciousness as a "self-knowing act" — the moment in which awareness turns back on itself and becomes aware of its own awareness. From physics, the same convergence: the gap is where consciousness recognises itself.

The Intelligence

Nine dimensions of
how your mind was moving.

LumeRI does not read the content of your writing. It reads the quality of mind that produced it. These nine psychological constructs — drawn from peer-reviewed research in self-compassion, interpersonal neurobiology, and narrative identity — are the lens through which your writing is seen. The analysis is language-aware and reads accurately across different English dialects.

C1
Cognitive Flexibility
Was the mind moving freely between possibilities, or locked into a single frame? Rigidity or openness to alternative perspectives.
Rooted in cognitive psychology · Hayes, 1999
C2
Emotional Intensity
How much weight was the writing carrying? Not good or bad — just how full. The measure of emotional presence in the session.
Affective neuroscience · Siegel, 2010
C3
Attribution Balance
Were things attributed to self, others, or context — and in what proportion? Where does the mind place the source of events?
Attribution theory · Weiner, 1985
C4
Reflective Depth
Was the writing exploring or reporting? Going under the surface or staying on it? The degree of genuine inquiry present.
Reflective functioning · Fonagy, 1997
C5
Narrative Coherence
Did things connect? Did events have causes, feelings have sources? The degree to which the mind is making sense of experience.
Narrative identity · McAdams, 2001
C6
Attentional Stability
Was the mind circling the same ground, or more settled? Rumination patterns versus groundedness in the present.
Rumination research · Nolen-Hoeksema, 1991
C7
Self-Relational Tone
What was the quality of the voice turned inward — warm, neutral, or hard? The self-compassion dimension of the writing.
Self-compassion framework · Neff, 2003
C8
Compassion Orientation
Was there care present — toward self, toward others, or both? The capacity to hold difficulty with warmth rather than judgment.
Common humanity · Neff, 2003
C9
Perspective Breadth
Could the mind hold more than one angle, or was it operating from one fixed point? The range of the mind's field of view.
Interpersonal neurobiology · Siegel, 2010

Questions

What people ask first.

Before you write your first entry. Before you see a reflection. These are the questions that come up.

Is this the same as a diary or journal app?+
Not at all. A diary records events. A mood tracker measures feelings on a scale. LumeRI does something different — it reads what you write and reflects back what it notices about how your mind was moving. Not what you said. How you were thinking. The constructs it tracks — emotional intensity, self-relational tone, reflective depth — are dimensions of your inner life, not your outer events. As for how often: there is no right frequency. Some people write daily. Some write when something is weighing on them. Five minutes or fifty. The only requirement is honesty. Patterns become visible regardless of frequency — because the signal is in the quality of attention, not the quantity of entries.
What is the Deep Lens? Where does it come from?+
The Deep Lens is a philosophical layer drawn from nine wisdom traditions spanning 2,500 years — Krishnamurti, Rumi, Ramana Maharshi, Nisargadatta, Lao Tzu, the Upanishads, and others. It is matched to the specific psychological pattern in your entry. Not advice. Not a prescription. A different quality of light on the same moment. Think of it as the mirror's second surface — where the ancient meets the personal.
Is my writing private? Who reads it?+
No human being ever reads what you write. Your entries are end-to-end encrypted, processed by AI to generate your reflection, and never stored in a way that any person — including our team — can access. You can delete everything, permanently, at any time. Privacy is not a feature. It is the foundation without which honest writing is not possible.
What is Reflective Intelligence — and how is it different from mindfulness or therapy?+
Reflective Intelligence is the capacity to observe your own thinking — to see a pattern before it becomes action, to question an assumption before it shapes a decision. Mindfulness focuses on present-moment awareness. Therapy works through relationship with a trained professional. LumeRI is neither. It is a private, daily writing practice with an intelligent mirror. No appointments. No vulnerability with another person. Just you, the page, and what becomes visible when you write honestly.
Will I see how I've changed over time?+
Yes. After several sessions, LumeRI generates a longitudinal summary — a narrative of how your inner patterns have moved, not a graph of metrics. You will see which themes your mind has returned to most, how your self-relational tone has shifted, whether your reflective depth has deepened. It is not a progress report. It is a mirror held at a longer distance — showing you the arc rather than the moment.

The Philosophy

There is a mirror in you. Not the mirror of reputation or self-image. The mirror of awareness itself — pristine, luminous, already whole.

And there is dust. Layer upon layer of conditioning — inherited belief, thought mistaken for truth, identity mistaken for self. It settles slowly, without drama, until the mirror is opaque and you have forgotten it was ever clear.

Seeing is what restores it. Not discipline. Not belief. Direct, choiceless awareness — the kind that does not add to the dust but simply lets it fall. What is seen clearly loses its grip.

The purpose of life, as understood here, is not to arrive somewhere. It is to let the mirror clear — and let the world flow through you. LumeRI was built from this understanding.
